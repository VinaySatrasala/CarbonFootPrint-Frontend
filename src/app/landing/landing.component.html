How we calculate ?

Transport

Water Usage

Water usage is calculated from household consumption patterns, including shower times, laundry habits, and appliance efficiency.

Waste

Waste emissions are calculated by analyzing the amount of household waste produced and how much of it is recycled.

Dietary Habits

Domestic Fuels

FAQ

Q1: What is a carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases emitted by human activities, typically measured in CO2 equivalent.

Q2: How do you calculate my carbon footprint?

We calculate your carbon footprint by analyzing your inputs in categories like transport, electricity, water, waste, domestic fuels, and dietary habits.

Q3: How can I reduce my carbon footprint?

You can reduce your footprint by using energy-efficient appliances, driving less, recycling more, reducing water consumption, and adopting a plant-based diet.

Q4: How accurate are the footprint calculations?

Our calculations provide a close estimate based on the data you provide and general emission factors for each category.

Q5: Why do dietary habits affect my carbon footprint?

Different foods have different environmental impacts. For example, producing meat generates more emissions than plant-based foods.

Q6: What can I do with my results?

Your results help you understand your impact on the environment and offer insights into how you can reduce your carbon footprint.

Q7: Why is transportation such a big contributor to carbon emissions?

Transportation, especially by car and plane, uses fossil fuels, which release significant amounts of CO2 into the atmosphere.

Q8: Can renewable energy reduce my carbon footprint?

Yes, switching to renewable energy sources like solar or wind significantly lowers your electricity-related emissions.
